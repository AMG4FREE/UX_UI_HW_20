WasteAway: A Mobile App

Problem: Recycling is hard

Solution: An App that helps you sort your recyclables.

View Final Prototype

Introduction:

WasteAway is an iOS app created to address the knowledge gap surrounding recycling information.

Our group felt that accessing consistent and useful information about recycling was scarce. After researching what was available on the Apple app store, it became apparent that not only is recycling and waste management a global issue, there simply aren't enough good resources available to help consumers make well-informed recycling choices.

Our goal is to provide a resource users can go to access recycling information that is useful, easy to understand and easy to use.

Research

The EPA began collecting and reporting data on the generation and disposal of waste in the United States over 35 years ago.
According to the Bureau of International Recycling,

“Recycling is a 200 billion dollar industry. Nearly 300 million tons of plastic waste is produced every year, only half of which is single-use.”

Bureau of International Recycling

Our goal is inspire waste reduction through education. We want to be the go-to resource for consumers to access accurate information on what products can and cannot be recycled.

Survey Results

In addition to our interviews, our user surveys further informed us that the rules around recycling aren't clear or straightforward across the board

Based on our research, we heard one consistent theme: Recycling is confusing. However, it doesn't have to be.

Problem Statement

“A person who struggles with recycling NEEDS a tool to provide them with useful recycling information BECAUSE improperly sorted recycling does not get recycled.”

User Insight Statement

Using our user insight statement as inspiration, we can see that our user is someone who feels frustrated with the complexity of recycling. They want to be good stewards of the environment and for their efforts to prove meaningful. They need a product that can drastically simplify the recycling process.

Stop wasting time and effort trying to figure out what goes in the recycling bin or the trash.

Waste Away: Recycle Right

WasteAway helps people who feel frustrated with the complexity of recycling by providing them with an app that has tools and resources to help them recycle correctly.

We know that people want to be good stewards of the environment and make an effort to recycle but the different rules and regulations that vary from city to city create barriers to entry.

WasteAway tells you exactly what kind of material you have and where it goes based on your area's specific recycling requirements.

Waste Away also provides users with resources to their local collection services and other sustainability organizations.

Prototype

After determining the features we wanted to have in our app, we began iterating on the prototype along with regular A/B testing.

Lo-Fi

After sketching out some preliminary wireframes, we built a low-fidelity prototype, establishing the initial UI elements we wanted to have and developing a simple user flow.

Lo-Fi Prototype
Mid-Fi

After developing the style of our document, we began adding these stylistic decisions to our mid-fidelity prototype. We designed with a flexbox framework in mind so that it’d be easy to align content and make it compatible on different smartphone screen sizes. Translating the Figma design into css had some compatibility issues which we made great progress toward cleaning up in later iterations. To test our mid-fi prototype, We added script tags in the head section of our HTML. We used HotJar to create a heatmap of our pages and Google Analytics to track different metrics of user behavior on the app.

Mid-Fi Prototype

Final UI Prototype

Based on feedback from our mid-fidelity prototype, we started building out our hi-fidelity prototype. During this process, we continued iterating on the page design and user flow using A/B testing.

We created different versions of the loading screen after a user takes a photo. We tested different versions of the home screen layouts. We also tested the content hierarchy of different card layouts.

In future iterations, we’ll add more features and create user flows for these.
